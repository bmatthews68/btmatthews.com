Professional Biography

Brian Matthews is a Technical Architect with over 20 years experience delivering solutions on C/C++ and Java based technology stacks for companies in the financial services, telecommunications, high technology, and other industries.

While completing his undergraduate degree in Computer Applications at Dublin City University, Brian started working with Polydata developing solutions in Pascal, C++ and assembly language for large petro-chemical companies such as ICI, . After leaving Polydata, Brian, spent the next 8 years plying his trade as a C++ contractor in Ireland, France, USA and UK working with AT&T Labs, British Rail, IBM, Hewlett-Packard, Lehman Brothers, Lotus, and Microsoft.

Brian joined International Financial Systems as the architect and assumed the role of software development manager.

After leaving IFS Brian joined Fidelity Investments as a Technical Consultant. At Fidelity, Brian was the product manager for Fidelity Online Xpress+ and later lead the largest of the development teams to build the replacement for FOX+ and PowerStreet Pro. Upon the completion of ActiveTrader Pro, Brian took on his first major Java project which re-architected PlanViewer for Fidelity International. Later Brian was seconded to KVH, in Toko, for 2 years as the Lead System Architect for a major OSS/BSS integration. Upon returning from his assignement in Tokyo, Brian briefly took on the role of .. governing the architecture for AccessCENTRAL and redesigning the provisioning system. Brian's last role at Fidelity was with TerraNua developing a multi-tennant software-as-a-service solution for hedge funds and registered investment advisors.

Since leaving Brian has resumed contracting and has worked as the technical architect for MyWeb at Vodafone. A developer at Corvil where he built high-speed decoders for market data and trading protocols in C/C++. A SOA architect at Fujitsu on projects for the Irish Courts Service, and the Department of Transport. A technical architect at D&B Business Information Systems on the data supply chain. Now Brian is working with LeasePlan IS helping them to re-engineer their out-dated Internet Quotations system.
